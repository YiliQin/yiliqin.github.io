I received the M.S. degree from the University of Tsukuba in 2019. Now, I am a Ph.D. student at the University of Tsukuba and research assistant in CNRS-AIST Joint Robotics Lab (JRL), Japan.

My research interests include whole-body motion planning and control of humanoid robots, robotic manipulation of long deformable objects like cables, belts, especially their applications in industry and real life.

  Research

    My Ph.D. research focusses on investigating the methods for manipulation of long deformable objects with humnaoid robot, including 1) perception and state estimation, 2) prehensile and non-prehensilbe manipulation, 3) manipulation planning in industrial assembly for a long deforamble object with 2D plane structure, e.g. belts and strips. The humanoid robot platforms we use are HRP-2Kai and HRP-5P.

    For my master's, I worked on robotic manipulation planning of long deformable objects assembly with 1D linear structure, e.g. cables and ropes.
